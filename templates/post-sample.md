# Working With the Garage Door Up — Week 00: Why I'm Doing This

_The "week zero" post. No code yet. Just the premise._

---

## The pattern I keep repeating

I start a project with a clear vision. Day one is the best day — the roadmap is crisp, the why is obvious, and I can hold the whole thing in my head at once.

Then I go heads-down to actually build it.

Six weeks later, I'm three levels deep in some detail that mattered a lot on Tuesday and I realize I've lost the thread. The roadmap I drew on day one is still sitting there, but it's a fossil now — it doesn't know what I've learned. And because it doesn't know, I stop trusting it. And because I stop trusting it, I stop looking at it. And now I'm just… coding. Without a compass.

Sound familiar?

## What I'm trying to change

I don't think the problem is that I zoom in too much. Zooming in is how things get built. The problem is that my zoomed-out picture is a **snapshot**, not something **living**. It gets taken once, at the moment I know the least, and then it just… sits there while I learn.

What I want instead: a big-picture view that I _nourish_ as I go. Something I re-touch weekly, not as a chore, but because it's where the project's soul lives. A doc that knows what I know today, not what I knew on day one.

## Why "garage door up"

[Credit: the phrase comes from Andy Matuschak's writing about working in the open.] The idea: instead of polishing something in private for months and then doing The Big Reveal, you work with the garage door open. People can walk by, peek in, see the sawdust. You narrate as you go.

I'm doing this for two reasons, one selfish and one less so:

1. **Selfish:** publishing forces the zoom-out. If I owe a weekly build log to an audience — even a tiny one, even just future me — I have to step back and make sense of the week. The habit I've failed to build in private might stick when it's public.
2. **Less selfish:** most of the software writing I learn from isn't the polished retrospective. It's the messy middle — people thinking out loud while they're still figuring it out. I want to add to that pile.

## The workflow I'm going to try

Three layers, three cadences:

- **Daily (5 min):** a commit log. What did I do, what did I learn, what surprised me. Raw. Private.
- **Weekly (30 min):** open the project's "north star" doc. Update it with what I learned. Rewrite the roadmap if it needs rewriting. Then: write this post. Publish Friday.
- **Monthly:** a bigger artifact — slides, explainer, whatever fits. Synthesize the month.

The north star doc is the hinge. One doc per project. It holds the vision, the current roadmap, and an append-only "what I've learned" log. I touch it every week. It never becomes a fossil because I never let it.

## What you can expect

Every Friday: a short post. What I worked on, what I learned, what shifted in the bigger picture. Some weeks will be interesting. Some weeks will be "I refactored the same function four times and hated it." Both are honest.

Garage door is up. Come look at the sawdust.
